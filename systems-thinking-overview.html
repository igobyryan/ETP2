Systems Thinking Tool

Finding Leverage Points: Reversing Vicious Cycles

Guide communities through a structured process to understand what's causing environmental problems and identify where doable interventions can create positive, self-reinforcing change.

About This Tool

When communities face environmental challenges like depleted fisheries, degraded forests, or water shortages, the problems are rarely simple. They're usually the result of interconnected causes that reinforce each other—what we call vicious cycles. A vicious cycle is when one problem makes another problem worse, which in turn makes the first problem worse, creating a downward spiral.

What This Tool Does

This tool guides you through a structured process to gather community knowledge, map vicious cycles, identify intervention points, evaluate which interventions make sense, and lock in gains through mutually reinforcing success.

Who This Tool Is For

Community groups, NGO practitioners, and local leaders facing interconnected environmental and social challenges.

Time Required

Multiple sessions over 2-4 weeks, depending on community size and complexity.

The Four-Part Process

This tool is organized into four interconnected parts that build on each other:

01

Recognize It

Identify the vicious cycles driving decline in your community. Learn to see the circular patterns of cause and effect that keep problems reinforcing each other.
02

Map It

Create a visual diagram of the feedback loops causing your problems. Bring diverse community voices together to understand the full story of decline.
03

Reverse It

Find leverage points where interventions can break negative cycles and start positive ones. Evaluate feasibility and choose your starting point.
04

Lock It In

Build mutually reinforcing success that becomes permanent. Create multiple virtuous cycles that support each other and integrate with community life.

Building on Community Knowledge

This tool works by bringing together diverse community knowledge—from elders who remember how things changed, to young people who understand current realities, to women who hold often-invisible knowledge. Together, you'll develop a shared understanding of your situation and find the leverage points for the most positive change.
